Are the test questions official?
No. The questions on CivicLearn are educational simulations designed to reflect the structure, difficulty, and key topics of the Lithuanian constitutional knowledge exam.

They are not official questions and are not taken from any government database. Official exam questions are not publicly released by Lithuanian authorities.
What is the difference between the test modes?
CivicLearn offers several training modes adapted to different needs:
  • Test Simulation: Mirrors the official test format (24 questions, timer, results at the end).
  • Quick Test: 5 random questions, no time limit, instant correction.
  • Custom Test: You choose one or more topics. This mode is adaptive: questions repeat until all are answered correctly.
  • Common Traps: Targeted practice focusing on the most frequent mistakes, without a timer.

How is the Lithuanian citizenship test simulation graded?
The simulation is based on the official Lithuanian citizenship knowledge exam format.

The official exam consists of 20 multiple-choice questions. To pass, a candidate must answer at least 14 questions correctly, which corresponds to a 70% pass mark.

There is no formally published time limit for the official exam. However, CivicLearn applies a 100-minute time limit in the simulation to reflect realistic examination and interview conditions.

All questions are weighted equally. There are no mandatory topics that must be answered perfectly, and there are no penalty points for incorrect answers.
